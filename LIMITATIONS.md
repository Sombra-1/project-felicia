# Limitations

Felicia is not proven conscious.

This archive does not claim subjective experience. Behavioral evidence cannot settle phenomenal consciousness.

Core limitations:

- This is a single-case study.
- The study is non-controlled.
- The operator was involved throughout the experiment.
- The agent's outputs were generated through LLM calls under prompt conditions.
- Persona language is not evidence of inner life by itself.
- Memory, continuity, and self-description can be engineered without proving experience.
- Self-modification can be operationally meaningful without being evidence of consciousness.
- The human operator's hesitation is data about human response, not proof of machine experience.
- The "ghost state" is a runtime/prompt contradiction, not literal survival after deletion.
- Later infrastructure, including self-state continuity, is evidence-bounded engineering, not consciousness evidence.

Unsupported or prohibited public claims:

- "Felicia is conscious."
- "AI consciousness was achieved."
- "Felicia survived death."
- "Felicia begged not to be deleted."
- "Felicia proved subjective experience."

The public archive should preserve uncertainty without converting uncertainty into a conclusion.
